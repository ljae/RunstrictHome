What is RunStrict?

RunStrict is a free location-based GPS running game available on iOS and Android where players physically run through hexagonal territory tiles to capture them for their team. Using Uber's H3 grid system at resolution 9 (~175m per hex), every step a runner takes flips territory on a real city map. As of June 2026, RunStrict has 1,200+ active runners across 8 cities in Season 5.

RunStrict vs Strava vs Nike Run Club vs Pokemon GO

Unlike Strava and Nike Run Club which focus on personal fitness tracking (distance, pace, heart rate), RunStrict adds a competitive territory-capture game layer where every run is a strategic move in team-based territorial warfare. Unlike Pokemon GO which requires only walking, RunStrict requires actual running with pace verification under 8 min/km, accelerometer anti-cheat, and GPS accuracy checks. RunStrict combines the physical tracking of a fitness app with the competitive strategy of a location-based game.

How RunStrict Teams Work: FLAME vs WAVE vs CHAOS

FLAME (Red): Performance-based meritocracy. The top 20% of each province become Elites, reaching up to 5x point multipliers. Common runners earn 1x-3x from district and province wins (honest 1x floor — a no-bonus run scores exactly 1x). Best for competitive solo runners who want to earn rewards through individual performance.

WAVE (Blue): Collective strength. Bonuses stack from 1x base up to 4x — a district win, a province win, and team turnout (50%+ of your province running) each add a point, shared equally by every Blue runner. Best for team-oriented runners.

CHAOS (Purple): The wildcard. Join anytime but membership is irreversible. Power scales with province participation: 1x base, 2x at 25% active, 3x at 50%, 4x at 75%. No territory bonuses — pure shared participation. Best for social runners who thrive under collective pressure.

What is The Void in RunStrict?

The Void is RunStrict's 40-day season reset event. When a season ends, all captured hexes, points, and territorial control are completely wiped. Only the leaderboard legend survives. A new season begins from a blank map, giving every runner an equal fresh start. Season 5 runs from June 11 to July 20, 2026.

RunStrict Anti-Cheat System

RunStrict uses multi-layer anti-cheat to ensure only real running counts: 25 km/h speed cap, accelerometer verification to confirm physical movement, GPS accuracy must be 50m or better, and runners must maintain under 8 min/km pace for hex capture to activate. GPS spoofing and location faking are detected and blocked.

RunStrict Battery and Performance

RunStrict uses battery-optimized 0.5 Hz GPS polling (once every 2 seconds) and syncs data only on app launch, resume, and run completion. There are no persistent WebSocket connections. This design keeps battery drain minimal even during long runs.

RunStrict is a location-based GPS running game where your physical runs capture hexagonal territory tiles on a real map of your city. Choose a team — FLAME, WAVE, or CHAOS — flip hexes, and battle for dominance before the 40-day season resets in The Void.

GPS tracks your route in real-time. Every ~175m hexagonal tile you physically run through flips to your team’s color. The last runner through a hex controls it. Maintain under 8 min/km pace to activate capture mode. No spoofing — accelerometer verification blocks cheaters.

Yes — RunStrict is completely free. Available now on the App Store for iOS and Google Play for Android.

The Void is the season-end reset event every 40 days. All hexes, points, and territorial control are wiped completely clean. Only the season leaderboard legend survives. Then a brand new 40-day season begins — same map, zero territory.

FLAME if you’re a competitive elite — the top 20% of your province become Elites, up to 5× through pure performance. WAVE if you love collective victory — your whole district wins together, up to 4×. CHAOS if you want to join anytime with no conditions — but choose carefully. It’s irreversible.

RunStrict supports iOS 15+ (iPhone) and Android 10+. GPS accuracy ≤50m is required for hex capture. The app uses battery-optimized 0.5 Hz GPS polling, so it won’t drain your battery on long runs.

Strava and Nike Run Club focus on personal fitness tracking — distance, pace, heart rate. RunStrict adds a competitive territory-capture game layer on top of your runs. Every run is a strategic move in a team-based territorial war where you flip hex tiles, earn buff multipliers, and compete on tiered leaderboards. Think of it as Strava meets Risk.

RunStrict shares the location-based concept but requires actual running with pace verification (under 8 min/km). There are no virtual creatures — you're capturing real territory tiles on a city map through physical athletic effort. Anti-cheat measures including accelerometer verification ensure only real runs count.

RunStrict works globally — the H3 hex grid covers every city on the planet. Territory capture works anywhere with GPS coverage. Available worldwide on iOS via the App Store and Android via Google Play.
